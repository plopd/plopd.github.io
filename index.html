My name is Daniel. I am a computer scientist.

One of my deepest interest in science is the development of artificial intelligence in the most general sense possible, adhering to simple natural laws of computation and information processing.

My broad interests in the topics of AI include:

  • Reinforcement learning: A computational approach to developing intelligent agents that can achieve goals in an unknown environment.

  • Continual learning is about systems that, once online and starting to learn, continue to do so indefinitely without ever being restarted or having their internal state changed by any external intervention. Forgetting things (to some extent) and learning them again when needed is fine, but losing the plasticity to learn is bad.

  • Meta learning: if the robot mind can learn continually, it surely will strive to learn how to learn better.

  • Recurrent neural networks are a fascinating mechanism for trying to construct an internal representation of the world as perceived by the computational mind from past experiences and current sensations.
